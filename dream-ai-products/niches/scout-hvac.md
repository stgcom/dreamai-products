## 4. Seasonal Patterns & Revenue Impact

### Demand Cycle
| Season | Demand Level | Revenue Impact | Marketing Opportunity |
|--------|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | 🔴 PEAK — AC demand | **40–50% of annual revenue** | "Beat the Heat" promos; Google Ads for "AC repair near me"; weather-triggered campaigns |
| **Winter (Dec–Feb)** | 🟠 HIGH — Heating | **25–30% of annual revenue** | Emergency repair plans; furnace tune-ups; pre-season maintenance |
| **Spring (Mar–May)** | 🟡 MODERATE — Prep | **15–20% of annual revenue** | "Spring Clean" duct checks; system inspections; pre-summer upgrades |
| **Fall (Sep–Nov)** | 🟡 MODERATE — Transition | **10–15% of annual revenue** | "Fall Furnace Check"; winter prep campaigns; maintenance plan sign-ups |

### Seasonal Business Dynamics
| Factor | Peak Season (Summer/Winter) | Shoulder Season (Spring/Fall) |
|--------|-----------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Revenue | High | Low (cash flow challenge) |
| Technician availability | Stretched thin | Underutilized |
| Ad costs (Google) | **$50–$150/click** (competitive) | **$15–$40/click** (bargain) |
| Customer urgency | High (broken AC = emergency) | Low (planned maintenance) |
| AI marketing window | Execute, don't plan | **Plan & build campaigns for next peak** |
| Team training | No time | **Best time to onboard new software** |

### AI Opportunity: Smoothing Seasonal Volatility
| Capability | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Weather-triggered ads | Auto-activate Google/Meta ads when temp exceeds/below threshold in service area |
| Maintenance plan automation | Proactively convert one-time customers to annual plans during shoulder seasons |
| Dynamic pricing | Adjust service pricing based on real-time demand + competitor activity |
| Demand forecasting | Predict workload 2–4 weeks out; optimize technician scheduling |
| Off-season marketing engine | AI generates seasonal content, email campaigns, social posts for slow periods |

## 6. Buying Cycle

| Phase | Duration | Actions |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Trigger Event** | — | New competitor opens nearby; can't hire; lost a bid; slow season pain |
| **Research** | 2–4 weeks | Googles "best HVAC software"; asks in Facebook groups; checks ServiceTitan |
| **Peer Validation** | 1–2 weeks | Asks buddy who runs another HVAC company what they use |
| **Evaluation** | 2–4 weeks | Tries free trial; tests on one or two techs; checks mobile app |
| **Decision** | 1–2 weeks | Owner approves; may involve office manager input |
| **Rollout** | 4–8 weeks | Tech training (resistance common); data migration; workflow adjustment |
| **Time to Value** | **60–90 days** | Must see scheduling efficiency or revenue lift |
| **Total Cycle** | **3–6 months** | Longer than med spa; relationship-heavy; skepticism requires proof |

### Best Times to Sell
| Timing | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Late Fall (Oct–Nov)** | Pre-winter prep; enough time to onboard before peak |
| **Early Spring (Feb–Mar)** | Pre-summer planning; shoulder season = time to learn new tools |
| **After a bad peak season** | Pain of broken processes is fresh; urgency is high |
| **After losing a key admin person** | "We need a system, not just Bob" moment |

### Worst Times to Sell
| Timing | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Mid-Summer (Jul–Aug)** | Too busy; can't train; owner focused on survival |

## 7. Beachhead Strategy

### Ideal First Customer Profile
- **HVAC service company, 5–20 technicians**
- **Annual revenue: $1M–$5M**
- **Location:** Sunbelt states (Texas, Florida, Arizona, Georgia) — higher AC demand = more seasonal volatility
- **Current stack:** Paper/dispatch board OR early ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro user frustrated by cost/complexity
- **Owner age: 40–60** (tech-averse but business-savvy)
- **Pain point:** Can't keep up during peak; hemorrhaging money during slow season; techs leaving for competitors

### Go-to-Market Wedge
1. **AI Scheduling & Dispatching** — smart routing, weather-aware demand forecasting
2. **AI Estimating** — instant, accurate quotes (beat EstimateKit on speed, ServiceTitan on price)
3. **Seasonal Marketing Engine** — AI content, weather-triggered ads, maintenance plan automation
4. **Pricing:** $79–$149/tech/mo (undercuts ServiceTitan by 60–70%; competitive with Housecall Pro)

### Why This Beachhead Wins
- **100,000+ companies** in US; extremely fragmented; no dominant software for small shops
- **ServiceTitan has a moat problem** — their pricing locks out 80% of the market; that's Dream AI's TAM
- **$847/mo average tech spend** means budget exists; just not being spent well
- **Seasonal volatility** is a universal pain — every HVAC company feels it, no tool solves it well
- **Tech resistance is the barrier, not budget** — if you make it dead simple, they'll switch
- **Winter-to-summer handoff** creates a natural annual review cycle (low switching risk once embedded)

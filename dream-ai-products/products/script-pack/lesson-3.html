Objection Handling

Turn "no" into "tell me more" — 5 common objections decoded

Lesson 3 of 560%

📖 In This Lesson

Objections are not rejections — they're buying signals. You'll master 5 scripts for the most common objections: price, timing, competitor loyalty, readiness, and brush-offs. Each script includes a psychology note explaining why it works.

🛡️
Objection Handling 5 Scripts
18
"That's Too Expensive"
When price is the primary concern
I completely understand — budget matters. What I can tell you is that most of our clients find the investment pays for itself within [timeframe]. Would it help if I walked you through what's included in the package? Sometimes seeing the full picture makes the value clearer.
Customize: Fill in [timeframe] with your ROI window (e.g., "the first two months"). If applicable, mention payment plans, tiered pricing, or a "starter" option.
19
"I Need to Think About It"
When they're interested but not ready to commit
Of course — it's an important decision, and I want you to feel completely comfortable. Would it be helpful if I sent you a summary or some resources to review at your own pace? I can also schedule a quick follow-up call whenever you're ready.
Customize: Never pressure. The goal is to keep the door open. Capture email for a follow-up sequence. Set a reminder in your CRM for 48 hours out.
20
"I Already Have Someone"
When they're with a competitor
That's great that you have a provider you trust. Just out of curiosity, are you completely satisfied with everything, or are there areas where you wish you were getting more? Sometimes a second opinion can reveal options you didn't know existed.
Customize: Be respectful of the existing relationship. Position yourself as complementary, not adversarial. If they express frustration, offer a no-obligation consultation.
21
"I'm Not Ready Yet"
When timing is the blocker
Totally fair. Timing is everything. When do you think you'd be in a better position to move forward? I can set a reminder to reach back out then, so you don't have to remember to call us. Would that work for you?
Customize: Capture a specific date or month. The AI should book a follow-up callback automatically. Add them to a long-term nurture email sequence.
22
"Just Call Me Back Later"
When they want to end the conversation
No problem at all — I don't want to take up your time. Can I get your name and the best number to reach you? And would you prefer a morning or afternoon callback? I want to make sure we connect when it's convenient for you.
Customize: Always leave with a commitment. "Afternoon works? Perfect, I'll have someone call you Tuesday between 1–4 PM." Specificity converts better than vague promises.

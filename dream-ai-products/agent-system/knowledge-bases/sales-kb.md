## 2. Common Objections + Responses

### Objection 1: "It's too expensive."
> **Response:**
> "I hear you. Let's look at it practically — what does one missed call cost your business? If you're in [industry], a single lost customer is worth $[X]. Our agents catch every call, 24/7. Most clients see ROI within the first week. Would you rather keep paying for missed opportunities at $0, or invest $[price] to stop losing $[X] every month?"

### Objection 2: "AI sounds robotic / My customers won't like it."
> **Response:**
> "Totally fair — that's the #1 concern we hear. Here's what we do differently: our agents are trained on YOUR brand voice, YOUR scripts, and sound genuinely conversational. We're not talking about those terrible IVR systems from 2010. Want me to show you a live demo so you can hear it for yourself? [Play demo]. Most clients tell us their customers can't tell the difference — and the ones who do are impressed."

### Objection 3: "I need to think about it."
> **Response:**
> "Absolutely, no pressure. Quick question though — what specifically would you need to think through? Is it the investment, the implementation, or whether it's the right fit? [Address the real concern]. The other thing to consider: every day without it is another day of missed calls and lost revenue. We can get you live in under a week. What would make this an easy yes?"

### Objection 4: "I already have a receptionist / front desk person."
> **Response:**
> "That's great — and they're probably amazing. But can they answer calls at 11 PM on a Saturday? Can they handle 15 calls at once? Our AI doesn't replace your team — it takes the repetitive, after-hours, and overflow work so your people can focus on the calls that really matter. Most of our clients pair both. Want me to walk you through how that looks?"

### Objection 5: "I've tried chatbots before and they were terrible."
> **Response:**
> "Same. Most chatbots ARE terrible — they're scripted decision trees from 2015. What we build is different. Our agents use actual AI — they understand context, remember the conversation, and sound human. Here's the proof: [show testimonial or play demo]. The question isn't whether AI chatbots work — it's whether you've tried the RIGHT one."

### Objection 6: "My business is too small for this."
> **Response:**
> "Actually, small businesses benefit the MOST. You can't afford to miss calls — every single one is real revenue. Big companies have teams to handle volume. You have... you. Our smallest client runs a solo HVAC company and says it pays for itself 3x over every month. The ROI doesn't care about your company size."

### Objection 7: "I don't have time to set it up."
> **Response:**
> "That's exactly why we exist. We do 100% of the setup — we just need access to your calendar, phone number, and a 30-minute call to nail your scripts. You'll be live within a week, and the only time you'll spend is approving the scripts we write for you. Sound fair?"

### Objection 8: "What if the AI makes a mistake?"
> **Response:**
> "Great question. Every conversation is recorded and reviewed. The AI is trained to transfer to a human when it's unsure. Plus, we build in guardrails — it won't promise discounts, share private info, or go off-script. And our clients review calls weekly (takes 10 min) to spot any issues. In practice, the AI makes far fewer mistakes than a rushed, overworked receptionist."

### Objection 9: "I need to talk to my business partner / spouse."
> **Response:**
> "Of course — big decisions deserve buy-in. What would your partner need to hear to get excited about this? [Listen]. Would it help if I sent you a one-pager with the ROI breakdown you can share? Or I could jump on a quick 15-minute call with both of you to answer any questions. What works best?"

### Objection 10: "Send me more information."
> **Response:**
> "Happy to. What specific info would be most helpful — pricing breakdown, case studies from [their industry], or a live demo? I'll send exactly what you need. What's the best email? And when should I follow up?"

## 4. Demo Scripts

### 4.1 Discovery Call Flow (15 min)
1. **Rapport (2 min):** "Thanks for your time, [Name]. Before I show you anything, I want to make sure this is a fit. Tell me about your business — what do you do, and what's a typical day look like?"
2. **Problem Identification (5 min):**
   - "How many calls do you get in a typical week?"
   - "How many do you think you miss?"
   - "What happens when someone calls after hours?"
   - "What's that costing you — in dollars or opportunity?"
3. **Demo (5 min):**
   - Live call to the demo agent (or recorded call from a similar niche)
   - Let the prospect experience it firsthand
   - "Notice how natural that sounded? That's what your customers would hear."
4. **Close (3 min):**
   - "Based on what you've shared, this would catch [X] missed calls/week. At $[avg value], that's $[X] in recovered revenue. Are you ready to get started, or what questions can I answer?"

### 4.2 Website Chatbot Demo Flow
1. **Show the widget on their site** (have their URL ready)
2. **Initiate a conversation** as a prospective customer
3. **Show lead capture** — "Watch, it just captured my name, email, and interest"
4. **Show appointment booking** — "And now it's booking directly into your calendar"
5. **Show the backend** — "Here's your dashboard. Every lead, every conversation, every booking."

### 4.3 Recorded Call Library (for email/text follow-up)
- Store 3–5 best recorded demo calls per industry
- Use in follow-up sequences: "Here's exactly what it sounds like for a [MedSpa/HVAC/RE] business"
- Always get prospect permission before sharing recordings externally

## 5. Closing Techniques

### 5.1 The Revenue Calculator Close
> "Let's do some math together. You said you miss about [X] calls per week. Average customer value is $[Y]. That's $[X×Y] per week in lost revenue — or $[X×Y×4.3] per month. Our agent costs $[price]/month. You'd need to recover just [1] customer per month to cover the cost. With [X] missed calls, you'll likely recover 10–20x that. Make sense?"

### 5.2 The Pilot Close
> "I get it — you want to see results before committing long-term. Here's what I'd suggest: let's do a 30-day pilot. We set everything up, you see real conversations, real bookings, real ROI. If it doesn't pay for itself in the first month, you walk. No risk. Sound fair?"

### 5.3 The Urgency Close
> "We're onboarding [X] clients this month and I've got 3 spots left for [their industry] before we close the books for onboarding. I can get you in this week if we move today — otherwise it's a 2-week wait. What's your calendar look like?"

### 5.4 The Competitor Close
> "I talked to a [competitor/similar business] in [nearby area] last week — they're already using this. The owner told me they've booked [X] extra appointments in the first month alone. I'm not saying you need to do what they're doing, but if your competitor is already using AI to capture leads you're not..."

### 5.5 The Break-Even Close
> "Let me ask you this: if I told you this would cost $[price] per month but generate $[price × 3] in new revenue within 90 days, would you do it? [Yes.] Great — then the only question is when you want to start."

## 6. Pricing Psychology Notes

- **Always lead with value, not price.** Discuss ROI before mentioning numbers.
- **Anchor high.** Start with the full DFY system price, then offer the standalone agent as the "affordable" option.
- **Monthly vs. Annual:** Offer 10–15% discount for annual prepay. "That brings it to $[X]/month, which is basically a cup of coffee a day for a 24/7 employee."
- **Payment terms:** Offer to start with a setup fee + first month. Reduces perceived commitment.
- **Never negotiate price.** If budget is an issue, adjust scope (fewer channels, less volume) — not price.

## 7. Qualification Framework (BANT+)

| Criteria | Questions to Ask |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Budget** | "What's your current monthly spend on reception/marketing?" |
| **Authority** | "Are you the decision-maker, or is there someone else who needs to weigh in?" |
| **Need** | "How many calls are you missing? What's the pain point?" |
| **Timeline** | "When do you want this up and running?" |
| **+ Tech Readiness** | "Do you use a CRM? Calendar system? What phone system?" |
| **+ Volume** | "How many inbound calls/chats do you handle per week?" |

**Lead Score:**
- **Hot:** BANT + Tech Ready + High Volume → Book onboarding call same week
- **Warm:** 3/4 BANT met → Nurture sequence + follow-up in 3 days
- **Cold:** <3 BANT met → Educational content + long-term nurture

### The AI Dispatch Center: How It Works

An AI dispatch center for HVAC isn't an answering service reading from a script. It's an intelligent system that understands emergency priority, qualifies calls, dispatches technicians, and schedules appointments — all without human intervention.

Here's the full system:

#### Component 1: AI Emergency Dispatch (The Money Maker)

**The priority matrix that makes everything work:**

🔴 **Emergency (Immediate dispatch)**
- No heat when outdoor temp is below 40°F
- Gas leak suspected
- Water/flooding from HVAC system
- Carbon monoxide alarm
→ On-call technician notified immediately via SMS/app notification
→ Customer gets: "We have a technician on the way. ETA: [X] minutes. You'll receive a text when they're en route."

🟡 **Urgent (Next-day priority)**
- AC completely out in summer (heat index 95°F+)
- Heating out (above 40°F outdoor temp)
- Unusual sounds, smells, or performance issues
- System cycling on/off repeatedly
→ Scheduled for next available priority slot
→ Customer gets: "We've scheduled you for [date/time]. Can't make it? Reply RESCHEDULE."

🟢 **Routine (Normal scheduling)**
- Annual maintenance/tune-up
- Filter replacements
- System inspection
- General questions
→ Scheduled during normal business hours
→ Customer gets: "We have availability on [dates]. Want me to book one?"

**The AI asks the right questions:**
1. "Are you experiencing an emergency — no heat, no AC, or a leak?" (priority detection)
2. "Is the system completely down or partially working?" (severity)
3. "What's your address?" (dispatch location)
4. "Is this a home or commercial property?" (service type)
5. "Do you have an existing service plan with us?" (existing customer flag)
6. "What's the best callback number?" (confirmation)

This takes 60–90 seconds. The customer feels heard and helped. The right priority is assigned. The right technician gets notified.

#### Component 2: Seasonal Booking Agent

**Peak season overflow management:**
When your phone lines are slammed during the first heat wave or cold snap, the AI handles overflow that your dispatch manager physically can't get to.

**Pre-season proactive outreach:**
- "Hi [Name], it's almost summer! Time to schedule your AC tune-up before the rush. Want me to book you a slot this week?"
- "Winter is coming — when was the last time your furnace was inspected? We have openings on [dates]."

**Why this matters:** Pre-season tune-ups are 70% margin work. Every tune-up booked is easy revenue. And proactive outreach builds the customer relationship before they need an emergency repair (and call someone else).

#### Component 3: Customer Follow-Up & Retention Agent

This is where most HVAC companies leave massive money on the table:

**Post-service check-in (24 hours after service):**
"Hi [Name], how's your [AC/heating] running after yesterday's service? Everything working properly?"

If positive → "Glad to hear it! Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? [Link]"

If negative → "We're sorry to hear that. A team member will call you within 2 hours to make this right." → Alert dispatch

**Maintenance reminders:**
- Monthly filter change reminders (for plan members)
- Annual tune-up scheduling: "It's been a year since your last system check. Want to schedule your annual maintenance?"
- Warranty expiration alerts: "Your [equipment] warranty expires in 60 days. Want us to do a final inspection while it's still covered?"

**Why this matters:** 60% of HVAC customers don't rebook maintenance. They forget your company exists until something breaks — and then they Google "HVAC repair near me" and call whoever answers first. Maintenance reminders keep you top-of-mind and turn one-time customers into $500–$1,500/year recurring revenue.

#### Component 4: Reputation Management

**Automated review solicitation:**
After every positive service interaction, the AI sends a Google Business Profile review request.

**Negative feedback interception:**
If a customer expresses dissatisfaction:
1. AI apologizes and offers to resolve
2. Owner/dispatcher alerted immediately
3. Customer calls back before they write a 1-star review

**The math on reviews:**
- Each 1-star review costs a local business ~30 customers (Harvard Business Review)
- Mediating one negative experience before it hits Google = $3,000–$10,000 in preserved future revenue
- 50+ Google reviews with 4.7+ stars = top 3 placement in local search results

#### Component 5: New Customer Lead Qualification

For customers finding you online (Google, Yelp, Nextdoor):
1. What's the issue? (AC, heating, indoor air quality, ductwork)
2. Is the system completely down or partially working?
3. When did it start?
4. Home or commercial?
5. Existing service plan?
6. Preferred date/time for inspection

Qualified leads flow directly into your scheduling system. Your techs show up prepared with the right parts and knowledge.

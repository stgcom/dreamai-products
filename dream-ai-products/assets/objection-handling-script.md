# Dream AI — Objection Handling Script

> **Purpose:** Equip sales reps with word-for-word responses to the top 15 objections when selling Dream AI to SMEs.
> **Format:** For each objection → the real concern → redirect technique → proof point → close attempt.
> **Usage:** Study before calls. Don't read verbatim — internalize the structure. Adapt to your natural voice.

---

## How to Use This Document

Every objection follows the **A.R.P.C. framework:**

1. **Acknowledge** — Validate their concern. Don't dismiss or argue.
2. **Reframe** — Shift from cost/risk to value/outcome.
3. **Prove** — Insert a specific data point or case study.
4. **Close** — Ask for commitment or next step.

**Golden rule:** Never say "I understand, but..." Always say "You're right — and here's what changes that."

## CATEGORY 1: PRICE OBJECTIONS

---

### Objection 1: "It's too expensive."

**Real concern:** They don't see the ROI yet. Price is always a proxy for perceived value.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "That's a fair reaction — let me reframe it. The Starter plan is $297 a month. That's about $10 a day. The average SME loses $8,000–$15,000 a month in missed leads — calls that come in after hours, web leads that sit for hours before anyone responds. If Dream AI captures just ONE additional job per month, it's paid for itself 10x over. I'm not asking you to spend $297. I'm asking you to stop losing thousands. Can we look at what a single missed lead costs your business?"

**Redirect technique:** Shift from expense to investment. Anchor against the cost of the problem, not the price of the solution.

**Social proof insertion:** "Our HVAC client was skeptical about $597/month until they realized they were missing $12K in after-hours jobs. Within 60 days, monthly revenue was up 34%."

**Close attempt:** "If I showed you the math on YOUR missed leads, would that change the conversation?"

---

### Objection 2: "We don't have budget for this right now."

**Real concern:** It's not a priority, or they need to justify the spend internally.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "Totally understand — budgets are tight for everyone. Here's what I'd ask: when you say no budget, is that a 'never' or a 'not this quarter'? Because the first month is free. Zero cost to try it. If it doesn't produce results in 30 days, you walk away having spent nothing. But if it captures even two extra jobs this month — which our clients typically see in the first two weeks — the budget conversation solves itself. Would a free trial make it easier to get internal approval?"

**Redirect technique:** Convert "no budget" to "no proof yet." Offer the free trial as the bridge.

**Social proof insertion:** "Three of our current clients started with the free month. All three are on the Growth plan now — because the math spoke for itself."

**Close attempt:** "If the first month is free, what's the actual barrier to trying it?"

---

### Objection 3: "I can hire a real person for less."

**Real concern:** They're comparing AI cost to a human salary — apples to oranges.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "You can — and here's the real comparison. A full-time receptionist costs $2,500–$3,500/month with benefits, taxes, PTO, and training. They work 8 hours a day, 5 days a week. They call in sick. They quit. Dream AI works 24/7/365 for $297–$597 a month. No benefits. No turnover. No training cycles. And here's the thing — we're not saying fire your front desk. We're saying redeploy them to higher-value work. Your receptionist should be helping patients, greeting clients, managing projects — not answering the same 10 questions 200 times a day. Dream AI handles the repetitive stuff so your team handles the important stuff."

**Redirect technique:** Compare total cost of employment (salary + benefits + PTO + turnover) vs. Dream AI's all-in price.

**Social proof insertion:** "One of our dental clients kept her receptionist but freed her up to manage in-office patient flow. No-shows dropped from 22% to 8%, and patient satisfaction scores went up."

**Close attempt:** "If you could get 24/7 coverage for under $600/month, does the math on a receptionist still work?"

## CATEGORY 2: TIMING OBJECTIONS

---

### Objection 4: "We're too busy to implement something new right now."

**Real concern:** Implementation feels like another project on an already full plate.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "I hear you — and that's exactly why this matters. You're too busy because your team is stuck doing tasks that should be automated. They're answering phones, entering CRM data, following up on leads. Dream AI takes all of that off their plate — and it takes 48 hours to set up, not 48 days. Your involvement is 10 minutes for the profile and 30 minutes for testing. We do the rest. You're not adding work. You're removing it."

**Redirect technique:** Reframe "too busy" as "too busy because you don't have this yet."

**Social proof insertion:** "A law firm we work with said the same thing. They went live in two days and their office manager told us, 'I finally have time to do my actual job.'"

**Close attempt:** "If setup takes less time than your next staff meeting, is there really a timing issue — or is this the right time?"

---

### Objection 5: "Let me think about it / I need to talk to my partner."

**Real concern:** They're interested but not ready to commit, or they need internal buy-in.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "Of course — take the time you need. But let me ask: when you talk to your partner, what questions do you think they'll have? If I can answer those now, it saves you a back-and-forth. And I'll send you a one-pager you can share directly — it covers everything we discussed in one page. The other thing I'll mention: our launch pricing locks in your rate for 12 months. If you wait and we raise prices — which we will — you'll pay more for the same thing. I'd rather get you in now at the best rate and let the product earn your renewal. Can I send you the one-pager and schedule a 10-minute follow-up for Thursday?"

**Redirect technique:** Surface the real objection by asking what the partner will ask. Offer to arm them for the internal conversation.

**Social proof insertion:** "I had a client who needed to convince his business partner. He sent them our case study — the HVAC company that added 34% revenue. His partner called me directly the next day."

**Close attempt:** "Let's schedule 10 minutes Thursday. That gives you time to talk to your partner, and I can answer any questions they have. Fair?"

---

### Objection 6: "I want to wait until things slow down."

**Real concern:** They think implementing this requires bandwidth they don't have.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "Here's the uncomfortable truth: things don't slow down. If your business is busy enough to miss leads, it's busy enough to need Dream AI. The companies that implement during their busy periods are the ones that actually see ROI fastest — because that's when the most leads are slipping through. Waiting until you're slow means waiting until there are fewer leads to capture. The best time to plug the leak is when the water's flowing."

**Redirect technique:** Flip the logic — busy = more leads to capture = faster ROI.

**Social proof insertion:** "Our fastest-paying client deployed during their peak season. They captured 47 missed calls in the first week. That's $18K in jobs they would have lost."

**Close attempt:** "What if we start the free trial now — no commitment — and you go live whenever you're ready? The clock doesn't start until you say go."

## CATEGORY 3: COMPETITOR OBJECTIONS

---

### Objection 7: "We already use [CRM / chatbot / answering service]."

**Real concern:** They have something in place and don't want to rip-and-replace.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "That's great — it means you already understand the value of the tools. Dream AI doesn't replace your CRM — it makes it better by filling it automatically. We integrate with HubSpot, Salesforce, GoHighLevel, Zoho, and Pipedrive. If you're using a chatbot, Dream AI works alongside it — we handle the voice channel and after-hours, your chatbot handles website visitors during business hours. And if you're using an answering service, you're paying $300–$600/month for someone who reads from a script and takes a message. Dream AI actually books appointments, qualifies leads, and syncs with your systems. It's not a replacement — it's the missing layer."

**Redirect technique:** Don't attack the current tool. Position Dream AI as complementary — the missing piece.

**Social proof insertion:** "A real estate client of ours was using an answering service. They were paying $450/month for message-taking. Dream AI replaced it, booked showings directly, and saved them $150/month while doing 10x more."

**Close attempt:** "If we integrate with what you already use — no rip-and-replace — what's the reason not to try?"

---

### Objection 8: "How are you different from [competitor X]?"

**Real concern:** They're evaluating options and need a clear differentiator.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "Good question — and I'll be straight with you. There are other AI receptionist tools out there. Here's where we're different:
>
> **First,** our voice AI sounds genuinely natural. Not robot-natural — actually natural. We've invested heavily in voice modeling, and most callers don't realize it's AI. Most competitors still sound like a 2015 IVR.
>
> **Second,** we're an AI employee, not just a receptionist. We do call answering AND speed-to-lead AND CRM auto-fill. Competitors typically do one of those three.
>
> **Third,** we're built for SMEs, not enterprise. No 6-month implementation. No six-figure contract. 48 hours to live. $297 to start.
>
> Try both. Call our demo line and call theirs. You'll hear the difference in the first five seconds."

**Redirect technique:** Don't trash competitors. Acknowledge them, then cleanly differentiate on voice quality, breadth, and speed of deployment.

**Social proof insertion:** "We've won deals from businesses that tried [competitor] first. Their feedback was always the same: 'Yours sounds more human and does more out of the box.'"

**Close attempt:** "I'll send you our demo number and a competitor's number. Call both. Let me know which one you'd want answering your calls."

---

### Objection 9: "I've heard AI tools are gimmicky."

**Real concern:** They've been burned by hype or have low-quality experiences to reference.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "You're not wrong — there's a lot of garbage out there. Chatbots that can't answer simple questions. Voice assistants that sound like they're from 2010. I get the skepticism. Here's how you know we're not that: call our demo line right now. Right now, on this call. I'll wait. Ask it anything — services, pricing, scheduling. See how it handles you. If it sounds gimmicky, hang up and we're done. But I think you'll be surprised. The reason our clients stay is because the product works, not because we sold them on a vision."

**Redirect technique:** Don't argue against the perception. Invite them to test it live — the product disproves the objection.

**Social proof insertion:** "We have a 94% retention rate after the first 90 days. Gimmicky products don't keep customers."

**Close attempt:** "Want to call the demo line right now? I'll give you the number."

## CATEGORY 4: TRUST OBJECTIONS

---

### Objection 10: "I'm worried the AI will say something wrong to my customers."

**Real concern:** Loss of control over customer interactions. Reputation risk.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "That's the right concern to have — and it's exactly what we built safeguards for. The AI operates strictly within your approved knowledge base. It doesn't improvise on pricing, services, or policies. If someone asks something outside its scope, it doesn't guess — it says 'Let me have someone from our team follow up with you on that' and transfers to a human with a full context summary. Plus, every interaction is logged. You can review transcripts at any time. You're never flying blind."

**Redirect technique:** Validate the concern as smart, then show the specific safeguards.

**Social proof insertion:** "We've had zero — zero — customer complaints about incorrect AI responses across our entire client base. The guardrails work."

**Close attempt:** "If the AI is constrained to your approved info and always escalates what it can't handle, what's the remaining risk?"

---

### Objection 11: "How do I know my data is secure?"

**Real concern:** Privacy, data sovereignty, compliance.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "Security isn't a feature for us — it's the foundation. Here's what I can tell you specifically: all data is encrypted at rest and in transit (AES-256 / TLS 1.3). We're SOC 2 Type II compliant. We don't train our models on your customer data — ever. Call recordings and transcripts are stored in your region and can be deleted on request. If you're in a regulated industry — healthcare, legal, finance — we can provide our full security documentation. I'll send it over after this call."

**Redirect technique:** Lead with specifics, not vague assurances. Offer documentation.

**Social proof insertion:** "We serve healthcare practices that require HIPAA compliance. Our security posture has passed every enterprise audit we've been through."

**Close attempt:** "If I send you our security whitepaper and SOC 2 report, would that check the box for you?"

---

### Objection 12: "What if Dream AI goes out of business?"

**Real concern:** Vendor risk — they don't want to depend on a startup that might disappear.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "Legitimate concern. Here's what I'll say: we're well-funded, growing fast, and our retention rate is 94% at 90 days. But I know that's what every startup says. So here's the real answer: your phone number and CRM integrations are yours. If you ever leave, you keep your number, your data, and your call logs. We don't hold anything hostage. We earn your business every month — and I'd rather be upfront about that than make you feel locked in."

**Redirect technique:** Acknowledge the real risk, then neutralize it by emphasizing data portability and no lock-in.

**Social proof insertion:** "We have clients who've been with us for over 18 months. The ones who stay do so because the ROI is real — not because they're stuck."

**Close attempt:** "If there's no lock-in and your data is portable, is the vendor risk actually a dealbreaker — or more of a 'nice to worry about'?"

## CATEGORY 5: COMPLEXITY OBJECTIONS

---

### Objection 13: "This sounds complicated to set up."

**Real concern:** They fear a long, painful implementation with IT involvement.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "I get that — and I want to show you exactly how simple it is. You fill out a 10-minute online profile — services, hours, pricing, FAQs. We build the AI on our end. You test it with a few calls, approve the script, and go live. Total time on your end: about 40 minutes spread across two days. No software to install. No IT team. No consultants. We've gone from sign-up to live in under 24 hours. If you can fill out a web form, you can set up Dream AI."

**Redirect technique:** Break down the setup into tiny, non-threatening steps. Emphasize what THEY do (minimal) vs. what WE do (everything else).

**Social proof insertion:** "Our record is 11 hours from sign-up to first live call. A plumbing company signed up at 9 AM and was capturing after-hours calls by 8 PM."

**Close attempt:** "If your active involvement is less than an hour over two days, is complexity really the blocker — or can we move forward?"

---

### Objection 14: "Will this work in my industry?"

**Real concern:** They think AI might be too generic or not specialized enough for their vertical.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "Dream AI is built to be customized to any business that gets calls and leads. We're strongest in home services — plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing — and professional services like law firms, accounting practices, and dental offices. But the platform is industry-agnostic because we train on YOUR business, not a generic template. Your pricing, your services, your availability, your tone. If a caller asks about root canals, they get a dental answer. If they ask about furnace repair, they get an HVAC answer. It's not one-size-fits-all — it's custom-built for you."

**Redirect technique:** Give specific industry examples to make it concrete. Emphasize customization over generic capability.

**Social proof insertion:** "We serve 14 different industries today — from roofing to real estate to veterinary clinics. The common thread: they all miss calls and lose leads."

**Close attempt:** "What's your business, and I'll tell you exactly how Dream AI would handle your calls?"

---

### Objection 15: "I'm not tech-savvy. Will I be able to use this?"

**Real concern:** Fear of complexity or needing technical skills to manage the tool.

**A.R.P.C. Response:**

> "You don't need to be tech-savvy. You need to be able to answer three questions: What services do you offer? When are you available? What should the AI say? That's it. We handle all the technical setup. Your dashboard shows everything in plain English — how many calls came in, how many were booked, how many leads were captured. No coding. No config files. No command line. If you can check your email, you can use Dream AI."

**Redirect technique:** Reduce the perceived skill requirement to zero. Use relatable anchors ("if you can check email").

**Social proof insertion:** "Our longest-tenured client is a 62-year-old electrician who describes himself as 'not a computer guy.' He checks his dashboard every morning over coffee. His words: 'I don't know how it works. I just know the phone rings less and the calendar fills up.'"

**Close attempt:** "If the most technical thing you do is read your email, are you tech-savvy enough for this?"

## Role-Play Training Scenarios

**Scenario 1 — The Skeptic**
Prospect: "I've heard this before. AI this, AI that. It's all hype."
Your move: Don't defend. Invite. "Call the demo line. Right now. If it's hype, you'll know in 30 seconds."

**Scenario 2 — The Price Shopper**
Prospect: "I found another solution for $99/month."
Your move: "What does that $99 solution do? If it's just a chatbot or a basic answering service, you're comparing a bicycle to a car. Ask them: does it book appointments? Does it fill your CRM? Does it sound human? If not, it's not the same product."

**Scenario 3 — The Busy Owner**
Prospect: "I'm slammed. Can we revisit next quarter?"
Your move: "Next quarter, you'll have the same amount of leads and the same missed calls. The only difference is another 3 months of lost revenue. The free trial means zero commitment — let's get you set up in 20 minutes today, and you can review results whenever you have time."

**Scenario 4 — The Committee Buyer**
Prospect: "I need to run this by my partner / team / board."
Your move: "Absolutely. What questions will they have? Let me answer them now so you have everything. I'll also send a one-pager you can forward directly — it covers the problem, the solution, the pricing, and the ROI in one page. When's the best time to follow up — after you've shared it?"
